Why OMOP at Emory

One data model. Multiple source systems. Global interoperability.

The OMOP Common Data Model is a global standard for organizing healthcare data. At Emory, we use it to unify records from Epic and Cerner into a single, research-ready data lake — so you can focus on discovery instead of data wrangling.

Because OMOP is the foundation of the OHDSI network, your cohort definitions, analyses, and phenotypes are portable to 400+ institutions across 40+ countries. Local insight becomes global evidence.
